Our vision

We believe spatial omics should be explored as a living map, not a static figure. SpatialViz aims to connect tissue morphology, cell identity and gene activity in one fast, shareable interface. Our goal is to help researchers move from overview to hypothesis faster: discover spatial niches, compare slices, trace molecular gradients and communicate complex tissue organization with clarity.

✦

Scale without friction

Multiple biological views

How to use SpatialViz

Open a database below, then use the floating controls to navigate, filter, color and inspect cells.

Choose a viewUse Spatial 3D, Spatial 2D or UMAP 2D to move between tissue structure and transcriptomic layout.
Select chip / sliceIn Spatial 2D, pick a single slice or move through slices with the slider.
Color by biologyUse Cluster Color for cell types or Expression Color to load a gene and view its spatial distribution.
Interact with legendClick legend entries to hide or show clusters; adjust point size and expression range for clearer patterns.
